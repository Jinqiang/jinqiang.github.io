Lingzhu Xiang

I am a PhD student at University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies, with advisor Professor Jonathan Kelly. My research focuses on visual-inertial navigation, multi-sensor calibration, and robust and active perception.

Previously, Master of Science (2015, Department of Computer Science, Brown University, advised by Professor Chad Jenkins). Bachelor of Science (2012, Department of Machine Intelligence, Peking University, advised by Professor Huijing Zhao).

I am a proud contributor to open source communities. Your Linux kernel probably has my code.

Projects

libfreenect2

I am a main contributor to libfreenect2, the open source drivers for the Kinect for Windows v2 device. I reverse engineered the calibrated depth decoding, and created various hardware acceleration (CUDA, hardware codecs) drivers for Intel, NVIDIA, and Tegra platforms. I also fix bugs and improve code quality. Find my upstream contributions, also check out my personal repo.

rosbeam

I reverse engineered Suitable Technologies' Beam telepresence robot. The rosbeam package provides motor control, odometry access, and camera feeds via ROS interfaces. My rosbeam repo at GitHub contains the code of the ROS package, and detailed documentation about Beam's internal communication protocols and structures. My Master's degree report (PDF) also discussed some details and applications.

PR2 human following demos

I developed human following demos of PR2 for RoboCup 2014. The work was later demonstrated for my advisor's talk at the World Science Festival 2014 (video, code).

Self-driving cars

With the POSS group at Peking University (2011–2012), I developed systems integrating motion planning algorithms with constraint of linear curvature and wheel turning controls realizing linear angular velocity, so that the planning is based on practical grounds and the actuation has fine guidance from higher level. Wheel odometry reader, control interfaces, networking API, and visualization were produced along the process.

Linux kernel

At Red Hat (2012–2013) I tested and patched a bunch of bugs among the UEFI subsystem. I also maintained code for several stable kernel branches. You can find some code or backports signed off by me on kernel.org, me on Bugzilla, or on mailing lists.
